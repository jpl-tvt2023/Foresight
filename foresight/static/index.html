ⓘ What each field means
Risk
Stockout = forecast demand will exhaust stock within the replenishment lead time. Overstock = cover beyond the healthy 45-day band, so storage charges accrue for nothing.
Item ID
Blinkit's item code (hover to see the full product name).
City
The dark-store city the demand comes from (in q-commerce the customer city ≈ the dark-store city). Hover the cell to see the state. “state-wide” rows are state stock that no city's demand claims — pure dead stock.
On hand
The city's estimated share of its state warehouse pool. Blinkit counts stock per state, so each state's on-hand is split across its cities by their share of the last 28 days' demand; after the last counted day the pool itself is ledger-reconstructed (Mode B).
Cover (d)
Days of cover: on-hand units ÷ forecast daily demand. How long the stock lasts if sales match the forecast. ∞ = stock present but no forecast demand.
Demand/d
Mean forecast units sold per day over the next 14 days.
Recommended action
What the balancing engine suggests — how many units to send in or recall.
₹ at stake
Stockout: margin you'd lose on the demand you can't fill. Overstock: storage cost you'd save, net of Blinkit's recall charge. Rows are ranked by this.
Source
Whether the forecast is measured (this cell's own history) or pooled (estimated from national volume — treat with more caution).

Days of cover — SKU × city

Each cell answers: “if sales match the forecast, how many days until this item runs out in this city?” Red = running out soon, blue = sitting too long, pale = healthy. Columns are grouped by state; hover any cell or column header for details.
ⓘ How to read this grid
Cell number
Days of cover = units on hand ÷ mean forecast demand/day (next 14 days). City on-hand is the state pool split by each city's 28-day demand share — an estimate, since Blinkit counts stock per state.
14–45 days
The healthy band — enough buffer to avoid stockouts without paying avoidable storage.
Red shades
Below 14 days of cover — the darker the red, the sooner it runs out.
Blue shades
Above 45 days — the darker the blue, the longer stock sits accruing storage charges.
∞
Stock is present but the model forecasts no demand — candidate for recall.
·
No stock and no demand in this city — nothing to do.
State ⁎ columns
State-wide pool with zero city demand in 28 days — dead stock that can't be attributed to any city; the clearest recall case.

Units by ageing slab (latest counted day)

Blinkit charges storage per unit per day, and the rate climbs as stock ages. Units drifting into higher slabs are the recall lever's target.
ⓘ What each field means
Ageing slab
How long the units have sat in the warehouse (e.g. 0–30 days, 31–60…). Higher slab = higher per-unit daily rate.
Units
Units counted in that slab on the most recent stock-count day.
₹/day
What those units cost in storage per day at current rates — the daily bleed if nothing moves.

Recall candidates

Overstocked cells where pulling stock back saves more in storage than the recall costs — ranked by net saving.
ⓘ What each field means
On hand / Cover (d)
Current units in that state and how many days they'd last at forecast demand.
Recall units
Units above the 45-day cover band — the excess worth pulling back.
Projected storage ₹
Storage you'll pay on that excess if you leave it where it is.
Net saving ₹
Projected storage minus Blinkit's recall handling charge. Positive = recalling pays for itself.

90-day demand forecast — day by day

One row per day: the model's best estimate plus an 80% range. Bands widen with horizon — near-term rows are plannable, day-90 is an envelope.
ⓘ What each field means
Forecast units
The model's point estimate of units sold that day.
Lo 80 / Hi 80
The 80% interval — on 8 days out of 10, actual sales should land between these. Plan safety stock off Hi 80, not the point.
measured / pooled
The chip above: measured = model trained on this cell's own sales; pooled = too little history, so it gets a share of the national forecast.

Units in storage

Daily on-hand units. Blinkit counts stock per supply state, so for a city this shows the whole state pool the city draws from.
units on hand per day — counted from ageing sheets; the tail after the last counted day is ledger-reconstructed (Mode B)
